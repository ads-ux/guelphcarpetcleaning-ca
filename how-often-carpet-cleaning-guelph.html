How Often Should I Have My Carpet Cleaned in Guelph?

Published 2026-04-15 — Guelph Carpet Cleaning

The standard recommendation for professional carpet cleaning frequency is every 12–18 months for most Guelph households. But your specific situation may call for more frequent cleaning — here's how to determine the right schedule.

Manufacturer Guidelines

Most carpet manufacturers recommend professional cleaning every 12–18 months to maintain the carpet's warranty. This isn't just a sales pitch — regular professional cleaning removes embedded grit and soil that acts like sandpaper on carpet fibres with each footstep. Carpets cleaned regularly last significantly longer than those cleaned infrequently.

Factors That Increase Frequency

  • Pets: Pet owners should clean every 6–12 months. Pet hair traps soil, and even house-trained pets have occasional accidents that require treatment before odour sets permanently
  • Children: Homes with young children see more food spills, tracked-in soil, and general traffic — every 12 months is appropriate
  • High foot traffic: Hallways, stairs, and living rooms with frequent use collect soil faster than bedrooms
  • Allergy sufferers: Carpets harbour dust mites, pet dander, and pollen — more frequent cleaning (every 6–12 months) measurably reduces allergen load for allergy and asthma households
  • Light-coloured carpet: Shows soil earlier; most homeowners clean before the year is up simply because it looks dirty

Guelph-Specific Considerations

Guelph homeowners face a particular challenge: spring mud season (March–April) when snowmelt and rain create significant tracked-in soil at entry points. Scheduling a cleaning in late spring (May or June) catches peak soil accumulation from the wet season. A second cleaning in fall (September or October) before winter addresses summer dust and prepares carpets for a winter of closed-window ventilation.

Vacuuming Between Professional Cleanings

Regular vacuuming significantly extends the effective life of a professional cleaning. High-traffic areas should be vacuumed 2–3 times per week; bedrooms and low-traffic areas weekly. Use a vacuum with a HEPA filter if anyone in the home has allergies or asthma. The goal is to remove dry soil before it bonds with carpet fibres and requires water extraction to remove.
